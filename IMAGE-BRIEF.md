# Techversity — Image Brief

**Goal:** Break the "empty typography" feel of the current site with a small set of deliberately placed photographs. The restraint is the point — we want the site to feel *editorial*, not *illustrated*. Ten to twelve images, well-placed, do more than thirty scattered stock shots.

**Global constraints — apply to every prompt below:**

- **Palette:** warm ivory / cream / ecru / bone / parchment dominant. Accents only in heritage-crimson (oxblood), deep Oxford navy, or aged gold. **No blue-grey tech tones. No neon.**
- **Medium:** shot as if on medium-format film (Hasselblad or Mamiya), not digital DSLR. Subtle grain. Natural light only. Soft shadows.
- **Mood:** unhurried, serious, considered. **Never cheerful, never stocky.** The subject is recognition of a life's work, not celebration.
- **People (where shown):** accomplished professionals aged 40–65. Styling: timeless, worn well — not young-startup, not corporate-headshot. Ethnic diversity represented naturally, never tokenistically. Often seen from behind, at profile, or hands-only. Faces rarely centered.
- **Avoid (critical):** graduation caps / mortarboards, handshakes, lightbulbs, pointing at charts, young models in suits, glass-and-steel office lobbies, stock-photo smiles, fluorescent lighting, cold blues, any text or logos visible in frame, any AI-generated sheen.
- **Aspect hints** are given per slot. Lean wider rather than taller — the layout uses horizontal spreads.

### A1 — Home · Pathways header
**Slot:** Above the 4 programme rows, full-bleed band, roughly 21:9.
**Intent:** The human behind *"four recognitions."* A distinguished professional contemplating work.

> An older professional in their late fifties, photographed from behind or at three-quarter profile, seated at a large wooden desk by a window in a warmly lit study. Soft afternoon light rakes across papers and a leather-bound portfolio in front of them. The subject is not looking at the camera. Wearing an unbranded dark wool jacket, no tie. Hands resting near a fountain pen. The room is quiet — walls the color of aged ivory, a single framed piece of art out of focus in the background. Deep shadows, warm ivory highlights, no cool tones. Shot on a 120mm medium-format film camera with subtle grain. Editorial magazine quality, quiet and considered. No text, no screens, no graduation regalia.
> `--ar 21:9 --style raw`

## Implementation notes

- **Where to put files:** `public/images/editorial/` for these photographs. Recommend names that match the slot (`home-pathways.jpg`, `dba-hero.jpg`, etc.).
- **Format:** AVIF or WebP preferred — run outputs through `next-image-export-optimizer` (already installed) for static-export compatibility.
- **Sizes to export:** at minimum `@1x` around 1440px wide for full-bleed bands, 960px for 4:5 portraits. Generate `@2x` where Retina fidelity matters (hero shots especially).
- **Colour grading after generation:** if generated images skew too cool, desaturate slightly then add a warm tint (temperature +10, tint +2) in any editor. The site's palette leans warm — any cool cast will read as foreign.
- **Do NOT colour-grade to match the crimson accent.** The accent is a UI element, not a photo tone. Images should stay neutral-warm; the crimson stays on the type.

## Ordering recommendation

If you only generate a few at a time, do them in this order — biggest impact first:
1. **A3** (Honorary Doctorate hero) — single highest impact, flagship page
2. **A4** (DBA hero), **A5** (PhD hero) — completes the programme page set
3. **A1** (Home Pathways header) — establishes the human on the landing

5. Priority B set — polish layer
